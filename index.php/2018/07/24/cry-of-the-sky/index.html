Cry of the Sky

by jsinghvi@blogger|Updated July 24, 2018

As I sit inside the warmth of my room, the raindrops on the window catch my attention. The storm has finally settled down after an hour of downpour, but my heart hasn’t. Maybe it’s just my imagination, but over the past few months, every time that I witnessed a rainfall, it felt like it was calling out to me. While everyone heard the thunder’s mighty roar, I heard an outcry. Like, it was trying to tell us the pain that we have caused it. How, even after the ruthless way we treat it – the pollution, the overpopulation, the wastage, fuel hunting to name a few – it still bears us with such kindness.  We’ve hurt our planet far too much to even have the right to call it ‘Mother Earth’.

Every day, every minute, we tread a path there’s no turning back from. And the most pitiful thing is that we’d rather turn a blind eye than do something about it. Now you might say “You’re wrong! Look there’s Tesla making electric cars, Denmark using renewable energy, people planting tress, yada yada..”, but ask yourself this – Is this enough to make up for the sins we’ve committed? for all the recklessness we evince everyday?

Our fate hangs by a thread, but we insist on staying ignorant. The future seems unclear, and honestly, it feels like soon we’re gonna turn from Masters to Scavengers.. Any hope for our Salvation? Maybe not. Or is there?
